PND (Post Natal Depression)

What is Post Natal Depression

Postnatal depression (PND) is a common problem. It was thought that about one in ten mums suffers from PND but many recent studies have indicated that it may affect as many as one in five. PND can sometimes be confused with the 'baby blues' which affects at least 80% of new mums and which are thought to be due to the huge hormonal changes that occur after birth.

However, whereas baby blues can resolve quite quickly, PND is unlikely to go away without the right support and treatment. Postnatal depression can include difficulties in bonding with your baby but this is absolutely not always the case.

Symptoms(1)

The experience of PND can vary a great deal but will include some of the feelings listed below.

  • A sense of hopelessness.
  • Guilty.
  • Exhausted.
  • Miserable.
  • Tearful.
  • Lonely.
  • Anxious.
  • Worrying thoughts.
  • Trapped.

Symptoms(2)

The counselling I offer can help if you are experiencing any of the following:

  • Feeling isolated in your new role as a parent.
  • Feeling sad and tearful.
  • Feeling anxious or afraid to be on your own with your baby.
  • Preoccupied with worrying thoughts.
  • Having difficulty adjusting to parenthood or in bonding with your baby.
  • Experiencing difficulties with your partner since having a baby.
  • Feeling stressed and unmotivated.
  • Feeling overwhelmed.
  • Worrying about returning to work after maternity leave.
