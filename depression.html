Depression

What is depression?(1)

Depression can ruin our relationships not only our own but the people who care about us too. Depression counselling and working on the underlying reasons for depression is the best way to live a happier life.

What is depression?(2)

Depression is the most commonly diagnosed of all the mental health issues, also closely associated with Anxiety, often both conditions go hand in hand. Depression counselling is the single most effective tool to improve mood and thinking. People often use phrases like “I’m depressed” to describe a temporary low mood, of feeling lost, hopeless, low, having no energy, or how they are feeling about a particular situation in their life. Depression counselling has proved to be an effective treatment.

In most cases, these low spirits lift of their own accord after a short period of time. However, if these feelings of unhappiness worsen and begin to interfere with how we live our everyday life, then it is possible that we may be developing a major or clinical depression. The word “clinical” simply means that the condition is severe enough to need some form of medical treatment which combined with depression counselling offers the best chance of improvement.

What are the symptoms of depression?

Depression can affect different people in many different ways. Depression counselling helps people explore and address the underlying causes for depression.

The following is a list of possible symptoms of depression but not all:

  • Feeling in a low mood for long periods of time.
  • Feeling anxious much of the time.
  • Finding it hard to concentrate.
  • Feeling unusually irritable.
  • Failing to derive pleasure from usually enjoyable activities.
  • Feeling tired and lacking energy.
  • Low self-confidence and self esteem.
  • Feeling numb and empty.
  • Feeling hopeless and helpless.
  • Feeling negative about the future.
  • Having problems getting to sleep.
  • Waking up early in the morning, or sleep problems.
  • Losing interest in sex.
  • Feeling guilty about things.
  • Feeling restless and agitated.
  • Being preoccupied by negative thoughts.
  • Having suicidal thoughts.
  • Having thoughts about harming yourself.
  • Experiencing physical aches and pains.

What can cause depression?

There is no one cause of depression. It varies from person to person. Broadly speaking there are three main triggers for developing depression:

Social Factors

Can include; losing your job, isolation, divorce, or bereavement can trigger depression in some people.

Psychological Factors

Prolonged anxiety, relationship problems, low self-esteem, childhood rejection or family background.

Physical Factors

Infectious diseases like flu or glandular fever; having a long-term physical health problem like multiple sclerosis; or as a side-effect of medical treatments like chemotherapy. It is also thought that some people may have a genetic tendency towards depression.

How Depression counselling can help and support you

If you are experiencing depression, you may feel unhappy, or feel low, that everything you do is a struggle, and perhaps not worth the effort. Often people experiencing depression feel hopeless about the future and unable to see any positives in life. You may feel flat and unable to participate in activities you used to enjoy. Sleeping problems may be an issue too.

At its worst, depression can lead to such feelings of helplessness and lack of self-worth that people may give up the will to live, or begin to have suicidal thoughts Working with an Online Counsellor offers people living with depression a more accessible service, no need to travel or leave your home. It is safe and secure and completely confidential, you can even remain anonymous if you prefer.
